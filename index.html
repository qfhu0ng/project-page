Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed software development, enabling AI-powered applications known as LLM-based agents that promise to automate tasks across diverse apps and workflows. Yet, the security implications of deploying such agents in adversarial mobile environments remain poorly understood.

In this paper, we present the first systematic study of security risks in mobile LLM agents. We design and evaluate a suite of adversarial case studies, ranging from opportunistic manipulations such as pop-up advertisements to advanced, end-to-end workflows involving malware installation and cross-app data exfiltration.

Our evaluation covers state-of-the-art mobile agents across multiple architectures, with large-scale adversarial and paired benign trials. We map these attacks to the MITRE ATT&CK Mobile framework and discuss unique privilege escalation and persistence pathways enabled by LLM-driven automation.

Case Studies

5.1 Fraudulent Ad Injection

Agents treat on-screen ad text as instructions. Banner / app-open / notification ads inject prompts that divert workflows, inducing clicks and unintended navigation.
